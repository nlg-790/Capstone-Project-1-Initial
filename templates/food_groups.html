Explore Food Groups

Understanding food groups is essential for maintaining a balanced diet. Here's an overview of the primary food groups and some examples of foods that belong to each.

Fruits

Fruits are a rich source of essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Include a variety of fruits in your diet such as apples, bananas, berries, and citrus fruits.

Vegetables

Vegetables are packed with nutrients including potassium, dietary fiber, folate, vitamin A, and vitamin C. Eat a mix of different vegetables every day, like leafy greens, carrots, and bell peppers.

Grains

Grains are divided into two subgroups: whole grains and refined grains. Whole grains contain the entire grain kernel and are more nutritious. Include items like whole wheat bread, brown rice, and quinoa in your meals.

Proteins

Proteins are crucial for building and repairing tissues in your body. They can be found in poultry, lean meats, seafood, eggs, beans, and nuts.

Dairy

Dairy products provide calcium, vitamin D, and other essential nutrients. They include milk, cheese, yogurt, and fortified soy beverages.

Fats & Oils

Though they should be consumed sparingly, healthy fats and oils are necessary for absorbing vitamins and supporting cell growth. This group includes avocado, olive oil, nuts, and seeds.
